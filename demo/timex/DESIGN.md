# Overview

This homepage is a vertically stacked ecommerce experience centered around luxury and lifestyle watch photography.

The visual system is intentionally restrained. The interface uses a predominantly black, white, and light-gray palette with very little decorative styling. Large product imagery carries most of the visual weight while typography remains minimal and functional.

The homepage alternates between:

- Large editorial hero moments
- Product merchandising grids
- Category highlights
- Lifestyle photography
- Promotional feature banners
- Product recommendation carousels

The design language favors:

- Large image surfaces
- Minimal borders
- Small navigation controls
- Clean product cards
- Wide white space
- Simple call-to-action buttons

The homepage feels product-first rather than interface-first.

---

# Key Characteristics

- Large full-width photography dominates most sections.
- Typography remains visually secondary to imagery.
- Predominantly black, white, and light gray color palette.
- Product cards repeat consistently throughout the page.
- Minimal use of decorative graphics.
- Product CTAs are compact rectangular buttons.
- Carousel controls appear as small circular icon buttons.
- Sections rely on spacing rather than borders for separation.
- Product imagery frequently bleeds edge-to-edge inside modules.
- Editorial storytelling sections are mixed between commerce modules.
- Footer switches to a fully dark theme.
- Watches are presented both as isolated products and lifestyle accessories.

# Do / Don't

## Do

- Use large watch photography.
- Keep typography restrained.
- Maintain monochrome UI surfaces.
- Allow imagery to dominate.
- Use generous spacing.
- Keep CTA styles consistent.

## Don't

- Introduce bright interface colors.
- Use heavy gradients.
- Add oversized decorative graphics.
- Over-style product cards.
- Add complex shadows.
- Use highly rounded modern SaaS components.
- Replace photography with illustration-heavy layouts.

---

# Iteration Guide

1. Preserve image-first storytelling.
2. Preserve monochrome UI hierarchy.
3. Keep typography secondary to products.
4. Maintain consistent product card structure.
5. Continue alternating editorial and commerce sections.
6. Keep call-to-actions compact.
7. Preserve generous whitespace around merchandising modules.
8. Use photography as the primary source of visual emotion.
